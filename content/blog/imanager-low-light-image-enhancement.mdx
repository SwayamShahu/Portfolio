## Abstract

This paper presents a comprehensive study on the design and development of systems for low-light image enhancement. Low-light photography suffers from high noise amplification, reduced colour saturation, and structural blurring — problems that degrade both human perception and downstream machine vision tasks such as object detection and segmentation.

The study surveys classical image processing approaches alongside modern deep learning methods, analysing their trade-offs in terms of perceptual quality, computational efficiency, and generalisability across different scene types. A novel enhancement framework is proposed that adapts to varying noise profiles without requiring scene-specific fine-tuning.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic comparative analysis of traditional (histogram equalisation, Retinex) vs. DL-based enhancement methods
- Proposed adaptive enhancement framework evaluated on LOL, MIT-FiveK, and custom datasets
- Analysis of perceptual metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) under varying ISO and aperture conditions
- Insights into real-world deployment considerations for mobile and edge devices
