## Abstract

Low-light image enhancement remains a challenging problem in computer vision due to complex noise patterns, colour distortion, and loss of structural detail in poorly lit scenes. This paper proposes a hybrid deep learning pipeline that combines **SwinIR-based denoising** with an **Attention U-Net** integrated with **Laplacian Pyramid Decomposition** to address these challenges.

The architecture decomposes images across multiple frequency scales, allowing the model to separately attend to structural detail and fine-grained noise. The SwinIR backbone leverages shifted window self-attention to capture long-range pixel dependencies, while the Attention U-Net decoder restores spatial detail with gated skip connections.

## Key Contributions

- A two-stage pipeline combining transformer-based denoising (SwinIR) with attention-gated segmentation-style upsampling
- Laplacian Pyramid Decomposition to separate high/low frequency components before enhancement
- Evaluation on standard benchmarks showing improved PSNR and SSIM over baseline methods
- Lightweight inference suitable for edge deployment scenarios
